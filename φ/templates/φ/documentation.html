VALUATION

Financial Ratios

The definition of the diverse ratios compiled are documented in footnote.

Capitalized Cash Flow

The Capitalization of Cash Flow Method is an income-based approach to valuation that is based on the company's ability to generate cash flows in the future. Diverse balance sheets adjustments are then executed to take into account the financial structure of the corporation. Again, the data is fully costumizable.

Capitalized Income

Capitalization of earnings is a method to determine the value of an business by calculating the worth of its profits based on current earnings and expected future performance. Considering the stage of the project, the information provided to you is only based on historical data. Feel free to adjust it to suits your need.

Capitalized Forecast

We provided you with a virgin capitalized cashflow template if you wish to evaluate the business based on forecast for which the data is not collected at the time being.

Capitalization Rates

A capitalization rate is used to compare different assets (businesses) on a mutual basis while also taking into account the specificities of each business in question. The rate usually considers the expected return, the growth of the corporation, its risk and the cost of it's total assets paid through interest and dividends.

Intrinsic Values

The intrinsic value page gives you an overview of the intrinsic value results of the different methods and gives you the opportunity to weight in these methods to suit your needs.

OPINION

Intrinsic Value

The intrinsic value from the Opinion section gives you the weighted intrinsic value based on the weight provided in the intrinsic value section and end up with a single intrinsic value for all common shares.

Market Capitalization

The market capitalization is the total market value of the common shares of a corporation that must be put in relation to the intrinsic value in order to asses the overvaluation (undervaluation). It is essentialy the product of the number of shares oustanding by the market value of each shares.

Valuation Ratio

The valuation ratio is the quotient of the intrinsic value over the market capitalization. It determines if the price of a common shares is expensive of not and is the final value concluding the decision making process.

Audit

The audit opinion is the opinion of the algorithm on the data is it provided with in the trial balances and the cash flow statements. In order to rely on the valuation ratio, one should make sure the underlying data is accurate and therefor make sure the audit leads to the opinion that nothing leads to believe that there are material misstatements in the financial statements.

Opinion

Based on the audit opinion and the valuation ratio, we conclude if the asset is one to buy or hedge for a loss in value.
