(a) Normalized Operating Cash Flow: Earnings before interest, depreciation, amortization and any activities that are not repeatable business functions, after reinvestement of maintenance.
(b) Weighted Importance: The relative importance given to each year in the cash flow normalization process.
(c) Normalized Weighted Operating Cash Flow: The sum of the product of each normalized operating cash flow by the weighted importance given to each period.
(d) Characteristic Cash Flow: Representative cash flow for valuation purposes.
(e) Capitalized Characteristic Cash Flow: The quotient of the representative cash flow by the capitalization rate.
(f) Excess (Insufficiancy) Working Capital: The excess of current assets over a factor of current liabilities required to maintain operating activities. By default the factor is set to 1.2 but might be different from one industry to another.
(g) Excess Assets: The value of assets not required for current operations, including investments and discontinued operations. By default, they constitute of investments and discontinued operations.
(h) Unrealized Capital Gain On Real Estate, Net Of Taxes: The non-materialized capital gain on assets that is not reflected in the costs and amortized costs of the financial statements.
(i) Present Value Of Tax Protection: Present value of future tax benefits. For reasons of availability of information, the present value of tax protection is valued at half the net deferred tax asset (liability).
(j) Business Value: Value of the company's total assets.
(k) Long-Term Debt And Operating Lease Obligations: The value of amounts due long-term to contributors of funds who are not shareholders of the company.
(l) Value Of All Category Of Shares: The net asset value of the company.
(m) Value Accruing To Preferred Shareholders: The amount that would have to be paid to reimburse the preferred shareholders in full.
(n) Common Shares Fair Value: Net asset value after considreation of value accruing to preferred and minority shareholders.
(o) Fair Value Of Identifiable Assets: The sum of commercial value of each asset that could be separetely disposed of.
(p) Goodwill Fair Value: Excess of intrinsic value over the fair value of net identifiable assets.
