(a) Target Working Capital: For the purpose of computing the Excess (Insufficiant) Working Capital (Capitalized Cash Flow & Capitalized Income calculations).
(b) Reinvestment Of Maintenance: Average payments to acquire property, plant and equipments (by default, it's the three years prior average).
(c) Theorical Interest Charge: 3.5% of debt, borrowings and obligations. We consider that any reduction from 3.5% would result in an external risk factor due to economic instability.
(d) Theorical Tax Rate: Tax charge for the year, provided that it is in the range of 15 and 30%. Anything lower would result in risk for tax positions and dependence on a jurisdiction. Anything higher is deemed attributable to the volatility of the effective tax rate between periods.
(e) Dividend Payment To Non Controlling Interests: Dividend payment to other than common shareholders are to be added back in the calculation of your cost of total assets for the purpose of finding your capitalization rate.
(f) Dividend Payment To Preferred Shareholders': Id.
(g) Total Assets Expected Return: Target return on all assets before factoring risk, growth and cost of financing otherwise accruing to common shareholders. By default, the expected return has been fixed to 4%. What is essential here is comparability.
(h) Capitalization Rates Floor: Lowest capitalization rate deemed reasonable.
