(a) Total Assets Expected Return: Target return on all assets before factoring risk, growth and cost of financing otherwise accruing to common shareholders. By default, the expected return has been fixed to 4%. What is essential here is comparability.
(b) Weighted Cost Of Total Assets: The average rate the company pays to all security holders to finance the total assets.
(c) Risk Related To Volatility Of Return On Total Assets: Standard deviation arising from the difference between return on total assets over the past five years and the average return on total assets for those same five years. Captures the risk that the yield will vary in the future based on past volatility.
(d) Expected Decline (Expected Growth): Average gross margin growth over the past three years.
(e) Cost Of Total Assets Accruing To Common Shares Shareholders: The part of the cost of total assets attributable non controlling interests and preferred sharesholders see Additional Information for normalization purposes.
(f) Growth Related Risk Provision: With respect to the Capitalization Rates Floor from Additional Information Tab.
(g) Capitalization Rates: The rates used to determine the multiple of characteristic or expected profit or cash flows. As default, the minimum value is 2.1% any additional growth results in an equivalent additional risk of volatility on total assets.
