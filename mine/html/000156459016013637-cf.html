- Definition

Represents the fair value of warrants issued in connection with debt that were initially accounted for as liability awards due to the variable exercise price adjustment feature (i.e., down-round protection) contained within the warrant agreement. Proceeds from the loan were allocated to the warrants and the debt on a relative fair value basis, resulting in a loan discount on the debt and a credit to other liabilities. The warrants were recorded at fair value for the period the warrants were classified as liabilities with changes in fair value recognized in other income and expense. Upon a subsequent round of equity financing, the variable exercise adjustment feature was extinguished and the warrant became indexed in the Company's own stock and were reclassified from liability to stockholders' equity. The reclassification of the fair value of the warrant from liability to stockholders' equity is a non-cash financing transaction.
